Good Visualizations

Good Visualizations

  • It seems that the author is just trying to communicate which regions have the highest rates of maternal deaths and investigate which areas of the world have the most severe mortality rate, which is done very clearly and well
  • This visualization makes effective use of the pie chart format in order to show the percentage breakdown of maternal deaths by region
  • The color palette is aesthetically pleasing, and although the shades of blue used fot Latin America and South Asia look similar, they are separated by the gray of East Asia/Pacific in order to prevent confusion
  • Each wedge of the pie chart is clearly labeled with the region name, quantity of maternal deaths, as well as the percentage
  • This visualization also has a clear title and disclaimer at the bottom to account for rounding
  • The graph is not overly complicated and has a low Lie Factor - that is, information from the data is not distorted by the visualization itself
  • There is no excess of ink or design present that obstructs or distracts from the data
  • There is no "chart junk," or excess information on the chart that is irrelevant
  • Reference: http://www.unicef.org/sowc09/statistics/charts.php
  • The author appears to be trying to convey a few ideas: (1) that the global under-five death rate has certainly gone down compared to what it would have been had 1990 levels been maintained, and (2) that there remains more work to be done in order to achieve the 2015 goal
  • Like the previous example of a good visualization, this graphic also has a clean format and color palette
  • This visualization has a clear key to distinguish the three types of lines on the plot and their individual implications
  • This visualization also has a low Lie Factor, as the y- and x-axes have a standard and consistent scale, and the y-axis is based at 0
  • This visualization also makes note of important points on the x-axis timeline, such as the date of the graph's creation (2013) as well as the original 2015 under-give mortality goal and projected attainment of that goal
  • The Data-Ink ratio is also acceptable - the visualization is a simple line graph, and has no excessive design or imagery so as to confound the actual data
  • Reference: http://www.bbc.com/news/health-29161620

Bad Visualizations

Bad Visualizations

  • The author is clearly trying to prove that the number of Americans on welfare exceeds the number of Americans who work full time in order to prove that welfare is putting a strain on the American economy
  • This visualization is decent in terms of color choice, but it is hugely misinformative due to the truncation of the y-axis
  • The Lie Factor of this visualization is incredibly high: on the scale of 100 million, a difference of less than 7 million should not create such a discrepancy between the heights of the two bars
  • This visualization also fails to take into account the frequent occurrence that people on welfare are also employed full-time, and instead views the two groups as mutually exclusive
  • This visualization could be much improved if the y-axis were based at 0 and clearly labeled in order to ensure that the difference between the two values is not exaggerated
  • It would also be worthwhile to explore visualization options that account for the overlap in the two categories, such as a Venn Diagram
  • Reference: http://mediamatters.org/blog/2013/10/28/dishonest-fox-chart-overstates-comparison-of-we/196618
  • It would seem that the author is trying to convey information about Americans' preference for particular types of pies by using a more "original" pie chart design and playing off the whole "pie" idea
  • This visualization may be fun to look at, since it uses images of actual pies for a pie chart, but the actual represented data is hugely problematic
  • The percentages do not add to 100, ostensibly because people "were asked to rank their three favorite types of pie;" however, that makes this pie chart both confusing and essentially useless, since pie charts are best used to portray percentages as they relate to a whole
  • While the pie images are cute to look at, they mess up the Data-Ink ratio, since very little real information is being portrayed with too much complex imagery
  • This visualization would be much improved if the documented "favorite types of pie" included only the top choice of those surveys - this would ensure that the percentages add to 100, and is more useful information than knowing people's top three flavors
  • Reference: http://www.livescience.com/33111-favorite-pie-america.html
